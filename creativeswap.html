CreativeSwap

A local artist trading platform connecting creatives within 50km to trade artwork, crafts, and services — no money involved, just creativity.

The Problem

Artists want to trade, but have no structured way to do it

Artists and creatives frequently want to exchange their work and services instead of paying cash — but currently rely on informal text messages and social media DMs, limited to people they already know. There's no centralized platform for local artists to discover trading opportunities, vet partners, or safely coordinate exchanges.

This creates a trust problem: how do you trade with a stranger safely? And a discovery problem: how do you even find someone nearby who wants what you have?

The Solution

A structured trading platform built around safety and community

CreativeSwap provides a full offer/accept/decline workflow, phone verification to prevent ban evasion, smart filtering by distance and creative medium, and real-time messaging that unlocks only after an offer is accepted. Safety features include public meeting warnings, a strike system, and a three-way trade capability for circular exchanges.

Trading System

Offer, counter-offer, accept or decline workflow. Three-way trades for circular exchanges. Real-time messaging unlocked after acceptance.

Safety & Accountability

Phone verification on signup. Strike system with permanent bans. Report system with admin moderation dashboard.

Smart Discovery

Filter listings by distance within 50km, creative medium, and what you're looking for in return.

Known Limitations & Reflections

What I'd do differently

Several architectural decisions made during early development created limitations that would require significant refactoring to resolve. The three-way trade system, while functional, lacks a structured data model to track which specific listings each participant is trading — meaning listing cleanup after completion is manual.

The trades completed counter was hidden due to a race condition where multiple clients fire the increment simultaneously — the correct fix requires a Firestore Cloud Function, which was outside the project scope.

These limitations were documented transparently and deprioritized in favour of delivering a stable, functional MVP. Given more time, I would implement Cloud Functions for server-side logic and redesign the three-way trade data model from the start.
